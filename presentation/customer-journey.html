Step 01 — Intake

A photo is enough to start

Most people don't arrive with a spec sheet — they arrive with a picture. A catalog photo, a dimensioned assembly diagram, a snapshot of a friend's wardrobe.

Joinery takes any of it straight in the design studio: JPEG, PNG, or WebP, no measuring tape required before you even start.

Step 02 — Reading

Dimensions, lifted off the drawing

A vision-capable model reads the photo the way a drafter reads a shop drawing — width, height, depth, door count, shelving.

It never invents a number it can't see. An illegible dimension gets left blank, not guessed.

Step 03 — The kernel

The model proposes. The kernel decides.

Every measurement the model reads is only a proposal. A deterministic parametric kernel checks it against real joinery limits — panel thickness, shelf span, minimum door width — and computes the exact geometry itself.

Same input, same output, every time. No hallucinated joints.

Step 04 — Preview

See it from every angle

The generated geometry renders instantly as an interactive 3D model — drag to rotate, scroll to zoom — with an AR view to stand it in the actual room.

It's the same 3D file that later drives the cut list and the assembly manual, so what you see here is exactly what gets built.

drag to orbit · scroll to zoom

Step 05 — Refinement

Nudge a number. It recomputes.

Widen it ten centimetres, add a shelf, drop the hanging rail — every change re-solves against the same constraints instantly.

Nothing is ever hand-patched geometry. It's recomputed from scratch, so it's always buildable.

Step 06 — Handoff

Off to a workshop that can build it

Confirming a design routes it to a manufacturer matched by capability — their tools, their materials, their maximum panel size — never a design they can't produce.

They receive the exact cut list and hardware count, not a rendering to interpret.

Step 07 — Verification

A miniature arrives before the real order does

The manufacturer prints a scaled prototype — staged inside a printed shell of your own room, walls and window included — a physical, in-hand check of proportions and fit before any full-size material is cut.

Only once that's verified does the piece move to production.

Step 08 — Delivery

One continuous thread, start to finish

Every stage traces back to the same validated spec — nothing re-entered, nothing re-interpreted. What you approved is what the workshop built, and what the workshop built is what arrives.
